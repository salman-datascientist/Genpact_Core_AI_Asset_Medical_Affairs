Static HCP Targeting — Not Dynamically Updated

What is HCP Targeting?

HCPs (Healthcare Professionals) = Oncologists, GPs, Specialists who prescribe Zejula. Medical Science Liaisons (MSLs) — GSK's field team — visit these doctors to share clinical data, answer medical questions, and understand unmet patient needs. Before visiting, they need a target list of which doctors to prioritise.

How the Static Target List is Built Today

January 2024: Analyst pulls Optum claims data for Q3 2023 Identifies 500 oncologists who prescribed PARP inhibitors Ranks them by prescription volume Creates: hcp_target_list_2024.xlsx This same list is used ALL YEAR by MSL teams — never updated.

Evidence Synthesis Bottleneck — SLR Takes 6–12 Analyst Weeks

What is a Systematic Literature Review (SLR)?

Before submitting to regulators or payers, GSK must prove they reviewed ALL available evidence — not just cherry-picked studies. This is an SLR — a rigorous, structured review of every published paper on a topic.

What a PubMed Search Returns

Search Query: ("ovarian cancer" OR "ovarian neoplasm") AND ("niraparib" OR "PARP inhibitor") AND ("real world" OR "observational") AND (2019:2024[dp]) Results: PubMed: 2,847 papers Embase: 1,903 papers After deduplication: 3,900 unique papers to screen

The Manual SLR — Phase by Phase

PHASE 1
Title/Abstract Screening
3,900 abstracts · 2 analysts · each abstract ~2 min
3,900 × 2 min = 130 hours
⏱ 3.25 analyst-weeks
PHASE 2
Full Text Review
400 papers pass Phase 1 · each paper ~45 min
400 × 45 min = 300 hours
⏱ 7.5 analyst-weeks
PHASE 3
Data Extraction Table
Fill extraction form for each of 400 papers
Author, year, n, outcomes, bias risk
⏱ 2 analyst-weeks
PHASE 4
Synthesis & Report Writing
Narrative synthesis of all included papers
Evidence tables, forest plots
⏱ 2 analyst-weeks
